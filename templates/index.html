Finger Print Authentication and Fake Sample Generation

Description

Since real biometric fingerprint samples fall under the category of personal data, their use in deep learning model training may be limited. Artificially generated fingerprint images are anonymous and can be used without restriction ("privacy-friendly"). Biological (papillary lines structure and change), forensic (computer fingerprint detection, reconstruction, and restoration of damaged samples), and technology studies are all interested in synthesised fingerprint samples (various methods of biometric security). It may be challenging to create fake fingerprints that perfectly imitate the textural qualities of real fingerprints. In this paper, we present the Adaptive Deep Convolutional Generative Adversarial Network (ADCGAN), a deep learning framework that we designed and investigated, and which has showed the potential to generate realistic fingerprint samples that are both realistic and accurate.
